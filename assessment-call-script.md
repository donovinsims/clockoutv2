# Assessment Call Script
*The exact questions to ask. The report to deliver. The follow-up that closes.*

---

## Before the Call

**Your goal:** Understand their operation well enough to identify 3–5 specific automations with dollar-amount ROI. Not to pitch — to diagnose.

**What to have ready:**
- Notepad or Google Doc open to capture their exact words
- The Pain → Cost Matrix (from ICP doc) so you can anchor numbers
- Calendar link to share for the follow-up call

**Framing when you book it:**
> *"This is a free 20-minute conversation about your business. I'll ask some questions about how your operation runs day-to-day, and within 48 hours I'll send you a written summary of 3–5 specific things I'd automate — with estimated ROI on each. No pitch. Just an honest look at where the holes are."*

---

## The Call Structure (20–30 min)

### Opening (2 min)

> *"Thanks for making time. I'm going to ask you some questions about how the business runs day-to-day — not the work itself, but the stuff around it. The admin, the follow-up, the calls, the scheduling. I'll take notes and put together a written summary for you within 48 hours. Sound good?"*

---

### Section 1: Business Snapshot (3 min)

> *"Just to make sure I have context — how long have you been running the business, roughly how many people are on your team, and what's the main type of work you do?"*

Listen for: team size, business age, service type. These calibrate your recommendations.

> *"What tools are you currently using to run the business? Anything for scheduling, invoicing, CRM, communication with customers?"*

Listen for: QuickBooks, Jobber, Google Calendar, spreadsheets, texting from personal phone. The more manual, the more opportunity.

---

### Section 2: The Leak (8–10 min)

This is the core of the call. Ask each question and then **shut up**. Write down their exact words.

**The main question:**
> *"What's the most annoying part of running the business day-to-day? The stuff that keeps coming back, the stuff that eats your evenings or weekends — what is it?"*

Wait. They will answer. This is your primary build target.

**Follow-up questions (pick 2–3 based on what they say):**

On leads:
> *"When a call comes in and you can't answer — what happens to that lead?"*

> *"How quickly do you usually follow up on new inquiries? What does that process look like right now?"*

On estimates:
> *"After you send an estimate, what do you do if you don't hear back? How many do you think go cold?"*

On reviews:
> *"Do you have a system for asking customers to leave a Google review, or does that happen manually?"*

On scheduling:
> *"When a customer no-shows or cancels last minute, what happens? How do you fill that slot?"*

On invoicing:
> *"When an invoice goes past due, what's your process for following up on it?"*

On admin:
> *"If I asked you what you spent your last Sunday evening working on — what was it?"*

> *"If you could hand off one thing tomorrow — one specific task you're doing yourself that shouldn't require you — what would it be?"*

---

### Section 3: Quick ROI Anchor (3 min)

Pick their biggest pain point and do the math out loud with them.

**For missed calls:**
> *"Rough ballpark — what's the average job worth for you? And how many calls do you think you miss in a week that don't call back?"*
> [Wait for answers, then]: *"So at $[their number] per job, even 2 missed calls a week that don't call back is about $[math] a month walking out the door. That's the number I'll be working against when I put the report together."*

**For no-shows:**
> *"If a tech shows up to a job that wasn't confirmed and the customer's not there — what does that cost you in time and wages?"*

**For estimate follow-up:**
> *"Of the estimates you send in a month, what percentage would you say actually close? And how many do you just never hear back from?"*

The goal: get them to say a number. Their number closes the deal — not yours.

---

### Close the Call (2 min)

> *"This is really helpful. I have a clear picture of where the biggest leaks are. I'll put together a written summary — what I'd build, estimated ROI for each, and a rough cost. I'll have it to you within 48 hours."*

> *"I'll also include a 4-day quick win plan — day-by-day steps so you can act on at least one thing immediately, even if we don't work together."*

> *"Does [their email] work for sending this over? And I'll include a link to book a 30-minute follow-up if anything in the report resonates."*

## The Assessment Report (Build Within 48 Hours)

Deliver this in Gamma, Google Slides, or a clean PDF. 6 sections.

### Section 1 — Executive Summary
Restate their 2–3 biggest pain points in their exact words. Not your interpretation — their words. This makes them feel heard before you've done anything.

### Section 2 — Effort vs. Impact Matrix
Draw a simple 2x2 grid:
- X-axis: Effort to fix (Low → High)
- Y-axis: Monthly value of fixing it (Low → High)

Map their pain points on it. The bottom-right quadrant (low effort, high impact) = **Quick Wins**. These are your sell.

*Example for a plumber:*
- Missed call text-back → Low effort, High impact ← Quick Win
- Review automation → Low effort, Medium impact ← Quick Win
- Full CRM setup → High effort, High impact ← Phase 2

### Section 3 — Recommended Solutions
For each Quick Win (2–3 max):
- **Problem:** (their words)
- **Solution:** (plain English, one sentence)
- **Tool/workflow:** (name it, note the cost — ideally free or <$30/mo)
- **Estimated ROI:** ($X/mo recovered or Y hrs/week saved)

### Section 4 — 4-Day Quick Win Plan
Make it dead simple. Day by day.

*Example:*
- Day 1: Set up Google review link. Send to last 5 customers manually.
- Day 2: Test the missed call text-back on your own phone number.
- Day 3: Review the estimate follow-up sequence I built. Approve it.
- Day 4: System goes live. Watch the first automated message send.

### Section 5 — What's Next (The Upsell Plant)
Name 1–2 bigger items that came up in the conversation — things you could solve together but that weren't in the Quick Win category. Don't pitch. Just name them and quantify why they matter.

*Example: "You mentioned scheduling chaos is eating 3–4 hours a week. That's a $600–$800 build that I'd prioritize after the quick wins are running."*

### Section 6 — Financial Impact Summary
Simple table:

| What | Hours/Week Saved | Monthly Value |
|---|---|---|
| Missed call text-back | — | ~$1,600 recovered |
| Review automation | 1 hr | ~$200 in lead value |
| Estimate follow-up | 2 hrs | ~$800 recovered |
| **Total** | **3 hrs/week** | **~$2,600/mo** |

*Tool cost: $0–$15/mo*
*Net impact: ~$2,600/mo in recovered revenue + 3 hrs/week back*

## The Follow-Up Call (30 Min)

Schedule this for 24–72 hours after you send the report.

**The structure:**
1. Screen share the report. Walk through each section.
2. Ask: *"Which of these resonates most with you?"* Let them pick the priority.
3. If you can, build it live during the call. Even a simplified version.
4. When it's working: *"This runs 24/7. Setup is $[X]. Want me to leave it running?"*

**If they're not ready to buy:**
- Put them in the email nurture sequence (7 emails, from Marketing System doc)
- Ask for a referral: *"Even if the timing isn't right for you — do you know anyone else who deals with this problem?"*

---

## After Your First 3 Free Assessments

Ask each person for:
1. A one-sentence written testimonial
2. One warm introduction to another business owner with a similar problem

Those two things are your entire pipeline for months 2–3.
