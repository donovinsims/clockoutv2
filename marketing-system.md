# Marketing System
*Vibe Marketing adapted for a local automation consultancy*
*Winnebago County, IL — Confidently boring. Local-first. Compound over time.*

---

## The Philosophy

> "Everyone's selling hype. You're selling compound growth. The boring angle is incredibly differentiated."

Your brand voice is already defined by the market around you. Every competitor is an offshore agency with a deck, a retainer, and a pitch full of buzzwords. You walk in, shake a hand, and fix something in front of them in 10 minutes.

**Lean into boring. Boring is the differentiator.**

---

## Brand Voice Profile

**Voice summary:** A smart neighbor who's figured out what actually works and is sharing it without the hype. Confidently boring — leans into the unglamorous stuff that holds businesses together.

| Use These | Avoid These |
|---|---|
| "Boring but it works" | "Revolutionary" / "game-changing" |
| "Runs while you sleep" | "Leverage" / "synergy" / "optimize" |
| Specific dollar amounts | "Unlock your potential" |
| "Fixed, fast, handed over" | "AI-powered transformation" |
| "I show up in person" | "Scalable solution ecosystem" |

**The three anti-hype angles (pick one per piece of content):**
- Local Installer: *"I'm not a tech company. I'm a neighbor who builds the systems your business should've had years ago."*
- Revenue Math: *"68% of missed calls never call back. How many jobs did you lose last month?"*
- Anti-Agency: *"I install it. You own it. I leave."*

## The Build Sequence

Run this once to establish your foundation. Everything after compounds from it.

```
RESEARCH → FOUNDATION → ASSETS → FLYWHEEL
```

### Stage 1 — Research (Do This Once, 2–3 hrs)

Run these searches before writing a single word of marketing copy:

- Google "HVAC contractor Rockford IL" — screenshot the top 5 results. What do they promise? What's missing? That gap is your angle.
- Search Google reviews for local contractors with <20 reviews — these are your first prospects AND proof of the problem you solve.
- Look at GoHighLevel, Jobber, ServiceTitan pricing pages — note what small operators complain about in reviews. That's your objection library.

**Save findings as:** `market-research.md` in your project folder. This feeds everything downstream.

### Stage 2 — Foundation (Voice + Positioning)

Already done via your business docs. Your positioning is:
- **Primary:** Anti-Agency (local, fixed price, no retainer, you own it)
- **Secondary:** Revenue Math (anchor every conversation to what the problem costs)
- **Tertiary:** Trusted Local Installer (show up in person, Winnebago County)

Your brand voice is: *Practitioner, not preacher. Direct. No tech speak.*

### Stage 3 — Assets (What You Actually Need)

**Priority order:**

1. **Website** (single landing page to start — see Claude Code prompt doc)
2. **Assessment report template** (Gamma or Google Slides — see Assessment Script doc)
3. **3 case study posts** (after your first 3 free assessments)
4. **Email sequence** (7 emails: deliver → connect → value → value → bridge → soft pitch → ask)

## The Traffic Strategy (Local-First)

For a Winnebago County consultancy, organic local beats SEO for the first 12 months. In order of ROI:

### Tier 1: Direct Outreach (Weeks 1–8)
- **Free assessments for your network** — 3 people you already know. This is your content and your referral base.
- **Door-knocking local businesses** — walk in with a problem and a demo, not a pitch. Easier than it sounds in 2025. You're not selling. You're having a conversation about whether they're leaving money on the table.
- **Local AI meetup** — host a free 20–25 min "AI for local businesses in Rockford" event. Use Luma or Meetup.com. You become the authority just by organizing it. Capture emails at the door.

### Tier 2: Community Presence (Weeks 4–12)
- **Local Facebook groups** — Rockford Business Owners, Winnebago County Contractors. Post the math, not a pitch.
- **BNI / Chamber** — one warm introduction from a happy client is worth 20 cold calls.
- **Office hours at a co-working space or realtor office** — "Free AI office hours, every other Thursday." Anyone can walk in and ask questions. Almost no downside.

### Tier 3: Content Compound (Month 2+)
- **LinkedIn** — one post per completed workflow. Math-first. Before/after. No tech speak.
- **Google Business Profile** — set yours up. Shows up in "automation Rockford IL" searches.
- **SEO** — one service page per workflow type ("missed call automation Rockford IL"). Low competition. Long game.

## The Consulting/Agency Content Formula

Every piece of content follows one of three formats:

**Format 1 — The Math Post**
> *"[Business type] was losing an estimated $[X]/month to [problem]. Here's the 5-minute system that fixed it."*
> Show the workflow. Show the before/after. Never explain the tech.

**Format 2 — The Objection Post**
> *"'That seems expensive.' Here's the math I show them."*
> Walk through the ROI calculation. Makes future prospects pre-close themselves.

**Format 3 — The Anti-Hype Post**
> *"You don't need an AI strategy. You need [specific system] that [specific result]."*
> Short. Direct. Links to your free assessment.

## Lead Magnet: The Free Assessment

The free assessment IS your lead magnet. It's low-friction entry, high-signal qualification, and the natural bridge to a paid engagement.

**Promotion copy:**
> *"Free AI Assessment for Rockford-area businesses. 20-minute conversation. I'll tell you exactly where your business is leaking time and money — and what it would cost to fix it. No pitch. Just math."*

Post this in local Facebook groups. Put it in your LinkedIn bio. It's your CTA on everything.

## Quality Gates (Before You Publish Anything)

From the Vibe Marketing playbook — apply to every piece of content:

- Would you actually say this to a Rockford contractor face-to-face?
- Is there a specific dollar amount or time savings mentioned?
- Does it sound like a neighbor, or a tech company?
- Is there one clear next step (usually: book a free assessment)?

If any answer is no — rewrite it.
