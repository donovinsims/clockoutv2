# Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
*Who I'm built for — and how to qualify them fast*

---

## One Line

**Owner-operated businesses across any industry doing $500K–$3M/year in Winnebago County — great at their work, running a 2004 back office in 2024. Trades and service businesses are the specialty lane.**

---

## Primary ICP: The Overwhelmed Operator

**Business type:** Any owner-operated business with phone, email, and back-office operational leaks. Specialty lane: trades and service businesses — contractors, HVAC, plumbers, electricians, landscapers, cleaners, roofers, pest control, auto shops. Also fits well: salons, dental offices, medspas, retail, professional services, and any local business where missed calls, cold follow-up, or manual admin costs real money.
**Revenue:** $500K–$3M/year
**Team:** 2–15 people (owner + staff) — the sweet spot is 5–15 employees
**Location:** Roscoe, Rockford, Beloit corridor, Winnebago/Boone/Stephenson County. Remote for strong fits.
**Business age:** 3–15 years. Past survival. Not yet systematized.
**Owner profile:** 35–55. Built it themselves. Uses their phone constantly. Probably on QuickBooks or a basic spreadsheet.

---

## What Their Day Looks Like

Answer texts. Chase a late invoice. Call back two missed leads. Update the schedule because someone no-showed. Remind a tech about a job. Respond to a review. Wonder why it's 9pm.

They're not lazy. They've just never had a system to get out of the loop.

---

## Pain → Cost Matrix

Use this during the assessment call. Get them to put a number on one item — that number closes the deal.

| Pain Point | Monthly Revenue Lost | Emotional Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Missed calls → lost leads | $1,500–$3,000 (68% never call back) | Frustration |
| Slow estimate follow-up | 30–40% of bids go cold | Anxiety |
| No-show appointments | ~$1,200–$5,000/mo depending on volume | Anger |
| No review system | Losing ranking to better-reviewed competitors | Dread |
| Owner buried in admin | 8–12 hrs/wk of high-value time gone | Burnout |
| Late invoice recovery | 15–25% of AR sitting 14+ days | Stress |

---

## Their Exact Words

> *"I miss calls and lose jobs to guys who answered."*

> *"I send an estimate and forget to follow up and they go somewhere else."*

> *"I spend half my Sunday doing stuff that shouldn't take more than an hour."*

> *"I tried some software but it was too complicated and I never used it."*

Mirror their language back in the assessment report. Not your words — theirs.

---

## What They've Tried (And Why It Failed)

- Hired an office manager — expensive, still have to manage them
- Bought a CRM, never fully set it up
- Used a call answering service — generic, impersonal
- Tried Zapier or Google automations — gave up in an hour
- Got pitched by a marketing agency — too expensive, too vague

**They're not looking for another tool. They're looking for someone to just fix it.**

---

## What Makes Them Buy

1. They see the **dollar amount** being lost by not having the system
2. The solution is explained in **plain English**, not tech speak
3. Setup is **fast** (days, not months) and the price is **fixed**
4. Someone **local and trustworthy** built it and stands behind it
5. The assessment already showed them exactly what they're getting before any money changes hands

They are **not** motivated by: being early adopters, "having AI," or abstract productivity gains.

They **are** motivated by: more jobs closed, fewer headaches, getting evenings back.

---

## Qualification Checklist

| Signal | Green | Yellow | Red |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $500K–$3M | $300–$500K | Under $300K |
| Team size | 5–15 people | 2–4 | Solo operator |
| Business age | 3–15 years | 1–3 years | Under 1 year |
| Specific pain | Names a problem with a number | Vague "things could run smoother" | No problem named |
| Prior tool attempts | Tried something, gave up | Never tried | Currently building themselves |
| Decision maker | Owner in the room | Owner + partner | Committee-managed |

**Two or more reds = bad fit. Move on.**

---

## Secondary ICP: The Growing Operator

Same profile, $2M–$5M, may have an office manager. Ready to systematize before hiring more. Better candidate for the full Operations Stack ($2,000–$3,000). Slightly longer sales cycle, higher LTV.

---

## Who Is NOT a Good Fit

- Businesses under $300K (ROI math doesn't pencil at that volume)
- Businesses with no meaningful operational leaks — no phone traffic, no estimates, no follow-up, no scheduling volume
- Owners who want to build/run the tech themselves
- Anyone needing marketing or lead generation (different service)
- Full CRM implementation or software migration (out of scope)
- Anyone without a specific named problem and a dollar amount they'd put on it

---

## Where to Find Them

**Fastest to start:**
- **Free assessments for your network** — 2–3 people you already know. Deliver the report. Ask for a testimonial and one warm intro. That's your first pipeline.
- **Door-knock local businesses** — easier than it sounds in 2025. You're not selling. You're having a conversation about whether they're using AI. Most aren't. Almost everyone will talk to you.
- **Local AI meetup** — host a free 20-minute "AI for local business in Rockford" event. Luma or Meetup.com. You become the authority just by organizing it.

**Ongoing:**
- **Google/Yelp searches** — businesses with few reviews have no review system. That's your entry point across any industry.
- **Local Facebook groups** — Rockford Business Owners, Winnebago County Contractors, and other local business groups. Post the math, not a pitch.
- **BNI / Chamber** — Winnebago County Chamber, Roscoe area. Business owners talk to business owners.
- **Referrals after every install** — *"Do you know two other business owners who deal with the same problem?"*
